# Story Elements Summary: Project "John Smith"

## 👤 Character: John Smith
- **Age:** 41.
- **Profile:** A programmer who uses an "Optimization Obsessive" mindset as a way to manage a neurodivergent (ASD) brain. 
- **Neurodiversity:** High-masking. He has spent decades perfecting a "neurotypical" persona to navigate social friction, which he perceives as "unstructured noise." This makes him appear "unremarkable" or "quietly professional."
- **Personality:** He seeks predictability and low-entropy environments. His "boring" nature is a defensive shell—a way to minimize sensory and social overwhelm.
- **Physical Environment:** His living space is "functionally messy." He does not prioritize physical order; his apartment is a collection of unorganized, low-priority clusters (laundry, mail, dishes). However, he possesses a perfect mental model of his environment, allowing him to navigate his "unstructured" space with high efficiency. His focus is on mental/intellectual order rather than physical tidiness.
- **History:** Twice married (both lasting less than four years). He attributes this to either his perceived "boringness" or a pattern of rushing into commitments too quickly.
- **Motivation:** Searching for a long-distance girlfriend (identity currently undefined). She is his "variable"—a probabilistic target in a broken world.

## 📍 Setting: Vancouver, BC
- **Primary Location:** The SkyTrain tunnels and Waterfront Station.
- **Atmosphere:** 
    - **Surface:** A brutal, record-breaking heatwave. Shimmering asphalt, oppressive humidity, and scorched earth.
    - **Underground:** A "non-place" of concrete and metal. Not pitch black, but a high-contrast, dim "grey" world with harsh shafts of light from ventilation grates and station entrances. The air is thick with dust and particulate matter.

## ☀️ The Event: The Solar Super-Flare
- **The Mechanism:** A massive burst of high-energy radiation (X-rays/Gamma rays) hitting Earth.
- **The "Kill":** Instantaneous death for those on the surface, leaving behind "Shadow People"—charcoal-black ash silhouettes scorched into the concrete.
- **The Communication Paradox:** Radiation arrives at the speed of light, causing an immediate radio blackout upon hitting the ionosphere. No warning can reach the population.
- **Electrical Impact:** 
    - Massive induced currents in long conductors (the SkyTrain's third rail).
    - Violent electrical surges and arc flashes.
    - Total grid failure causing fail-safe brakes to slam shut.

## 📖 Plot Progression (Opening)
1. **The Build-up:** John is in the Waterfront Station tunnels during a heatwave, managing the sensory "noise" of commuters.
2. **The Strike:** Electronic failure $\rightarrow$ Electrical grid screams $\rightarrow$ Blinding flash $\rightarrow$ SkyTrains screech to a violent halt.
3. **The Diagnostic & Failed Warning:** John realizes the nature of the event (CME/Solar Flare). He attempts to warn others to stay underground for at least 72 hours to avoid radiation, but his warning is dismissed. His delivery—slow, hesitant, and lacking "social confidence"—causes the panicked crowd to ignore him. Consensus only forms later as casualties mount.
4. **The Scavenge:** John manages his "resource allocation," gathering high-density, predictable provisions (water, protein bars) and sensory anchors from station kiosks and staff nodes.
5. **The Discovery:** John ascends to the surface after the 72-hour window passes to find a sterilized city of "shadow people."

## 🛠️ Workshop Areas / Open Questions
- **The Girlfriend:** Who is she, and what "data fragments" of her does John hold?
- **The Goal:** Is he searching for her, or is he searching for the "logic" behind why the world broke?
- **The Environment:** How long can the tunnels support life before the heat and lack of air make them tombs?
- **The Survivors:** Who else is left in the "sub-systems" of the city?
